Rector, AR is home to two historical and beautifully maintained parks, the Rector Memorial Park and the Rector Downtown Central Park.

Rector Memorial Park

Adjoining Memorial Park on the south is Rector’s softball/baseball complex, which includes four fields and a rodeo arena. These facilities serve both local residents and visiting teams during tournaments.

Over the years, Memorial Park has seen numerous improvements, including landscaping, reforestation, and the planting of 64 new trees to replace those lost to disease and high winds. Streets within the park have been paved, and street lighting has been installed.

The park features four flower beds maintained by the Rector Garden Club, as well as two playgrounds with modern equipment that meets state and federal safety standards. Underground wiring and coaxial cable connect to all stands, allowing volunteers to bring their own TVs during events such as the annual Labor Day Picnic.

Three RV pads are available with full hookups: $17 per night for 30 amp service and $27 for 50 amp, payable in advance. Reservations can be made by contacting City Hall. Contact City Hall

Rector Memorial Park has hosted the annual Labor Day Picnic since 1941. The park land was donated to the city around 1937 by former businessman Alfred West.

Rector Downtown Central Park

In the center of town is the Rector Downtown Central Park a beautifully landscaped small park with a bandstand and a memorial walkway. Benches and trees make it an inviting place to rest. The memorial brick walkway is the primary source for financing the needs of the small park. In 1912, this little park was a rather unsightly, place for hitching horses when people were visiting the train station. The Rector Woman’s Club was formed in that year and made this muddy mess its first project. Keeping with one of their goals the Rector Woman’s Club continued to maintain and decorate the Park until the club disbanded. After that the Rector Beautification Committee volunteered to oversee the upkeep of the Downtown Central Park.
